1. Getting Started

System Requirements

  • Recommended specifications:
    • Windows 7 or later with x64 (64-bit) CPU architecture
    • 1GHz or faster CPU
    • At least 4GB RAM
    • Microsoft .NET 4 (which is available for Windows XP SP3/Windows Server 2003 SP2 and later versions)
  • Minimum specifications:
    • Windows XP SP3 or later with x86 (32-bit) or x64 (64-bit) CPU architecture
    • 1GHz or faster x86/x64 CPU
    • 1GB RAM for x86-architecture systems OR at least 2GB RAM for x64-architecture systems
    • Microsoft .NET 4

Overview

Yahtzee! is a luck-strategy game in which players score points by rolling a set of five six-sided dice.

Objective

The objective is to score as many points as possible from the dice combinations rolled. The player with the highest score at the end of the game is the winner.

2. Gameplay

Introduction

Yahtzee! is a game that employs both chance and strategy. By rolling a set of five cubic dice, it is up to you how you wish to award your own points based on the combinations you roll.

Adding/Removing Players

After you start the application, you will be greeted with a dialog box in which you can create your player roster. This roster will allow for you to add between 1 to 10 players. Each player must be added with a unique screen name. To add a name to the roster, press the [+] button to the right of the input box; likewise, to remove a name, press the [-] button. To begin the game, press the Play! button. The application will then generate a score sheet and initialize a new game for you.

NOTE: It may take a moment to generate a score sheet. However, the more players you add to your roster, the longer it will take for the application to do so.

Turn-Based Gameplay

The score sheet will be displayed as a dual-axis table, with the first five letters of each player's name displayed across the first row (which we'll call the "queue") and the category labels displayed along the first column.

A player's turn will be indicated by a light yellow column beneath the currently active player's name on the score sheet. Once the active player has used his/her turn, it will rotate to the next player in the queue. After the last player in the queue has used his/her turn, it will return to the first player in the queue. This cycle will repeat until every player has filled every category in his/her column, at which point the game will declare the final winner(s).

Rolling the Dice

For each turn, the active player will be given three opportunities to roll the dice, of which five are provided. The number of rolls remaining will be indicated in the caption of the Roll Dice button in the dice roller toolbar at the top.

If you see a pattern respective to any of the categories you are aiming to fill, you have the beneficial option to hold dice to prevent them from being rolled. You can hold or unhold a die by clicking on it. A held die is indicated by a blue background color with white dots, while an unheld die is indicated by a white background with black dots.

Category Selection

Before concluding one's turn, a player must decide which category to fill based on the dice value combination he/she has rolled. Information on how to effectively choose a category can be found in the Scoring section of the documentation.

You cannot select a category before using your first roll. Once you are ready to select a category or have exhausted all your rolls, you can select a category by clicking the Category button in the dice roller toolbar at the top. A small dialog will appear with a dropdown list of all the categories you have not yet used. Click OK to finalize your selection.

If you are playing in a multiplayer setting, your turn will rotate to the next player in the queue once you have selected a category.

Scoring

Your score tally will depend on your dice value combination and what category you select based on that combination. If your selected category does not in any way relate to the combination you have rolled, a score of zero (0) points will be awarded to that category and you will not be able to use that category again during the current game.

The following information outlines how many points you can earn from the categories you choose:

  • UPPER SECTION
    • ACES: Adds one point for every 1 rolled.
    • TWOS: Adds two points for every 2 rolled.
    • THREES: Adds three points for every 3 rolled.
    • FOURS: Adds four points for every 4 rolled.
    • FIVES: Adds five points for every 5 rolled.
    • SIXES: Adds six points for every 6 rolled.
  • LOWER SECTION
    • 3 OF A KIND: Adds the total of all dice.
    • 4 OF A KIND: Adds the total of all dice.
    • FULL HOUSE: Adds 25 points for a 2-of-a-kind and a 3-of-a-kind.
    • SMALL STRAIGHT: Adds 30 points for a sequence of 4.
    • LARGE STRAIGHT: Adds 40 points for a sequence of 5.
    • YAHTZEE: Adds 50 points for a 5-of-a-kind.*
    • CHANCE: Adds the total of all dice.

UPPER SECTION TOTAL: If you score 63 or more points in your Upper Section Subtotal, an additional 35-point bonus will be awarded to your Upper Score Total.

LOWER SECTION TOTAL: * Per traditional game rules, for every subsequent Yahtzee you roll, an additional 100-point Yahtzee Bonus will be awarded to your Lower Section Total. However, Yahtzee Bonus is not currently available in this version of the software.
